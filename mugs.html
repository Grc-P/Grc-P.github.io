Mugs

Always a little too small or too big

Mugs of all shapes and sizes

Not to sound too pretentious but I think there is something really special about a handmade mug. They have so much character and you discver something new about their form each time you use them. This is a far cry from the lifeless design of mass produced mugs. Here are some of the mugs I have made so far:

The Matcha Mug

Felt a bit mugged off when I saw the colour of this one after glaze firing. It was supposed to be a beatiful blue colour(see middle test tile on the glazing page) but instead came out black. It is growing on me though, I like the shape and it looks pretty cool with my matcha in it, like a potion in a cauldron or something.

The Tooth Brush Holder

This was meant to be a mug but it was too thick and heavy and I also let it dry too much before I added a handle to it. It is discouraged to join together pieces of clay with different levels of moisture in them as this can lead to uneven shrinking of the clay and cracking. It is now instead a tooth brush holder in bathroom blue.

The Little Green Mug

My favourite mug and one of the first I made. A thing of beauty. Dedicated to the best coconut shy player in Yorkshire, and probably the world.
